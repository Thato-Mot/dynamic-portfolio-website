Bird Call Classification Using Machine Learning
University of Cape Town
Machine Learning
Investigating machine learning techniques for the automated classification of bird species based on their vocalisations. The project aims to support biodiversity monitoring and conservation through advanced bioacoustic analysis.
My role involved:
  • Feature Engineering: Extracting and analysing features like MFCCs, Mel spectrograms, and Chroma features.
  • Model Development: Evaluating traditional models (e.g., Random Forests) and deep learning architectures (e.g., CNNs).
  • Data Augmentation: Implementing techniques such as pitch shifting and noise addition to enhance model robustness.
  • Evaluation: Testing models under real-world conditions to assess generalisation and performance, achieving:
    - Random Forest Accuracy: 72.3%
    - SVM Accuracy: 74.4%
    - VGG-16 Transfer Learning Accuracy: 89.2%
Star/Delta Starter Circuit Analysis and Modification
University of Cape Town (Machines Laboratory)
Panel Wiring
Analysed, modified, and tested a star/delta starter panel for reducing starting current in induction motors. Improved efficiency, corrected wiring issues, and ensured proper functionality through schematic design and rewiring.
My role involved:
  • Schematic Design: Produced accurate circuit schematics for both the unmodified and modified panels.
  • Panel Modification: Rewired and labelled all connections, improved efficiency by reordering components, and ensured electrical isolation between control and power circuits.
  • Testing and Troubleshooting: Performed continuity tests, identified issues like improper coil voltages, and implemented solutions including a step-up transformer.
  • Results: Achieved a fully functional control circuit and optimised panel wiring for future testing and modifications.
